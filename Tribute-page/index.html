The People's President: A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

On the solemn occasion of the death anniversary of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, former President of India, we pay tribute to a remarkable individual whose life and legacy continue to inspire millions across the nation.

Abdul Kalam's Life Journey

  • 1931: Born on 15th October in Rameshwaram,Tamil Nadu
  • 1954: Graduated from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • 1955: Enroled at MIT to study aerospace engineering
  • 1960: Joined DRDO as Chief Scientist
  • 1965: Started working on Rocket project independently in DRDO
  • 1969: Transferred to ISRO
  • 1980-90: Developed AGNI and PRITHVI missiles
  • 1981: Awarded Padma Bhushan
  • 1990: Received Padma Vibhushan
  • 1992-99: Served as Chief Scientific Adviser to the PM and Secretary of DRDO
  • 1997: Honored with Bharat Ratna
  • 2002-07: Served as India's 11th President
  • 2012: Launched "What Can I Give Movement" for youth
  • 2015: Passed away on 27 July in Shillong, Meghalaya

"If you want to shine like sun, first burn like a sun"

-A.P.J. Abdul Kalam
